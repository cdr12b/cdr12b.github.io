Abstract
For this interferometry experiment we attempted to find the angular size of the
sun by measuring the power ouput in the radio frequency range. Our reults
were inconclusive but possible good results would show the angular diameter of
the Sun to be about .5◦ ± 1’. This shows that by using fringe patterns of the
amplitude of the power and knowing the baseline of the interferometer we can
find the angular size of an object from space.

waves deconstruct. The purpose of our experiment was to take us through the

Procedure/Methods
For this lab we began by setting up the circuit board that was connected to the
arduino. We spent the first few weeks working on coding and learning how to
set up the interferometer. The coding was needed in order to use the arduino
to read the voltage obtained by the LNB in order to log the data to a file. This
process could return very large amounts of readings during short periods of time.
During a test to see if we could read in data from the LNB while in the lab we
assumed there was a short circuit since the power supply to the circuit board
stopped working and broke a piece of the circuit. So in order to take readings
from the Sun we used pen and paper.Our first test was to use a small dish
setup that was just a dish without mirrors. We used this small dish to test our
equipment and see if we could measure any fringes without the interferometer.
We measured the voltage spread using a satellite reader . After testing using
the small dish we moved onto the interferometer. We set up the interferometer
by pointing it directly at the sun. Then we swept it across the Sun from about
±20◦. From here we took readings at different baselines. We would rotate a
degree and then take a measurement of the voltage from the satellite reader.

Conclusion
For our experiment we did not find the visibility due to not finding fringes in our
data. Even though our experiment was inconclusive, with proper information
we could calculate the angular diameter of the sun. Theoretical predictions
show the angular size of the sun to be about .5◦ ± 1’ .By using the underlying
physics scientists can use telescopes miles away from each other to measure the
angular diameter of objects extremely far away from earth.
